**Problem 1.** A consumer has utility

$$
u(x, y) = \min\{\, 2x,\; x + \ln y \,\},
\qquad p_x, p_y > 0, \quad \text{income } M.
$$

1. Find the kink locus and sketch a representative indifference curve.
2. Derive Marshallian demand $(x^d, y^d)$, identifying the corner, interior,
   and kink regimes and the conditions on $(p_x, p_y, M)$ under which each
   obtains.
3. Verify your answer at $p_x = p_y = 1$, $M = 2$ using the convex-preferences
   visualiser.

**Problem 2.** Consider

$$
u(x, y) = \min\{\, 3x^2,\; xy,\; 3y^2 \,\}
= \begin{cases}
3x^2 & \text{if } y \ge 3x,\\[4pt]
xy & \text{if } \tfrac{x}{3} < y < 3x,\\[4pt]
3y^2 & \text{if } y \le \tfrac{x}{3}.
\end{cases}
$$

1. Solve the expenditure minimisation problem for target utility $\bar{u} > 0$
   and derive Hicksian demand $(x^h, y^h)$ in each price regime.
2. State and verify the duality relation $e\bigl(p_x, p_y, v(p_x, p_y, M)\bigr) = M$.
